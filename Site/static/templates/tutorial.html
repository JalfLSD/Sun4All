Sun4All Tutorial

Hello!

First of all, thank you so much for giving us your time to help us with Sun4All! This tutorial will maximize your experience and contribution, so please, read it carefully.

The project is based on an asset of over 30000 images of the Sun (spectroheliograms) that are kept at the Astronomical Observatory of the University of Coimbra, as result of a work of over 80 years of daily solar observations, started in 1926.

The analysis and counting of the sunspots is a very important aspect of the study of the phenomena occurring on the surface of Sun and to understand the solar activity and solar cycle. On the other hand studying the sunspots, besides being interesting itself, presents an important tool to understand much of what happens on the Earth climate.

Thank you for your collaboration!
In this application you’ll find an image of the sun - a spectroheliogram - dated with the day when was taken. The one used in this example was taken in September, 03 – 1980 (see above the picture).

The purpose of this application is to count sunspots and sunspots groups. To do that, we provide you with tools to manipulate the image and to do the counting.

You can use icon to get more information about the application and also the social network icons to spread the word!

To manipulate the image, you have several buttons located at the bottom of the image that you can use:
To change the size of the image you can:
Amplify , reduce , or reset the size of the image .
To move the image you can:
Move the image down , up , left or right .
You also can INVERT THE IMAGE and detect sunspots easily.
To do the counting, you have located at the right side of the image two boxes:
The “Observation” box will help you analysing the image by allowing you to “Add Sunspots”, “Add Sunspot Group” and “Add Information” that you may find relevant.
The “Your Data” box resume your analysis by telling you how many sunspots and sunspot groups you have counted so far and also, allows you to “Finish the task” and to “Start over” the analysis.
To count sunspots or sunspot groups just follow the next steps:
1) First you have to create a square around the area you want to cover. To do this, click on the image using the right button of the mouse.
2) Then, you can adjust the square to your spot easily by cliking in the square and dragging him to the position you want.
3) Finally, you have to go to the “Observation” box and click on “Add Sunspot” button which will mark your spot with a yellow circle!
For sunspot groups, the process is the same but in this last step you must click on “Add Sunspot Group” button which will mark your sunspot group with a blue circle.
Once you have counted every sunspots and sunspot groups in the spectroheliogram, you just have to click on the “Finish Task” button!

Now you are ready to start contributing! You can also check on the didactic unit.

Thank you so much for your time and have fun!
